# Daily Business Brief

> Step 9 of the guide. Start the day with clarity instead of drift.

---

## The prompt

Paste into the **Strategy** Project (with `about-me.md` + `brand-voice.md` attached) each morning:

> Read `about-me.md`, `strategy.md` and my outreach log.
>
> Write me a tight morning brief:
> 1. **Top 3 priorities today.** Ranked, and each one has to move the 30-day goal (paying clients now). If a priority is "build something," challenge it.
> 2. **Most urgent follow-up.** Who's waiting on me, and how long have they been waiting.
> 3. **One thing I'm probably forgetting.**
>
> Keep it under 200 words. Don't be encouraging, be useful. If yesterday's priorities didn't get done, say so and ask why before adding new ones.

---

## Why the "don't be encouraging" line matters

Default output is agreeable. An agreeable brief tells a solo founder that building another landing page is a great use of Tuesday.

The documented failure mode here is **building assets instead of finding customers**: ~20 tools, 11 posts, a full marketing kit, and zero Google results. The brief exists to interrupt that, not to cheer it on.

---

## The standing question

Every brief should be able to answer:

> **Did anything I did yesterday put money closer to the door?**

If the honest answer is no for three days running, that's the finding. Surface it.

---

## What to feed it

The guide's step 9 assumes Connectors pulling from Notion/Drive/Slack. Vanorika doesn't run those. What actually exists:

- **`about-me.md`**: the goals and constraints
- **`projects/strategy.md`**: the open strategic questions log
- **Outreach log**: manual. Domain, date, finding used, channel, replied y/n.
- **`donovanmudarikwa@gmail.com`**: every inbound lead lands here via Web3Forms, source-tagged (e.g. `Homepage scanner (host: 42%)`). This is the closest thing to a CRM.
- **Calendly**: booked consults

**The outreach log is the missing piece.** Without it the brief is guessing about the pipeline. A single spreadsheet or markdown table is enough. It does not need to be a CRM.

---

## The weekly version

Fridays, same Project:

> Review the week against the 30-day goal in `about-me.md`.
>
> - How many prospects did I actually scan and contact? How many replied? How many converted?
> - Which finding types got replies, and which got silence?
> - What did I build this week, and was building it the best use of that time, honestly?
> - What's the single highest-leverage thing for next week?
>
> Then update the open questions in `strategy.md` with anything that got resolved.

## Suggested outreach log format

| Date | Business | Domain | Finding used | Channel | Replied | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-07-15 | Example Ltd | example.co.zw | No HTTPS on checkout | WhatsApp | Y | Report sent |

Thirty rows of this is worth more than any marketing asset in the repo, because it's the only thing that tells you which finding actually makes a Harare business owner reply.

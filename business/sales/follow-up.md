# Follow-Up Sequence

> For warm leads who haven't replied. Step 5 of the guide.
> Voice rules in `brand-voice.md` apply.

---

## Who this is for

Someone who engaged and went quiet:

- Ran a scan on the site and submitted their details at the gate (name + WhatsApp + email)
- Replied to a cold opener, then stopped
- Took the free report and didn't come back
- Had the consult and never signed

**Not** for people who never responded at all. If a cold opener got nothing, send one follow-up and move on. Chasing silence burns the number and the brand.

---

## Principles

1. **Every follow-up adds something.** New finding, new context, a relevant post, a deadline that's actually real. Never "just checking in," because that's a message that says *I want something from you and have nothing to offer.*
2. **Space them out.** Day 3, day 10, day 30. Not daily.
3. **Three, then stop.** After the third, close the loop and leave the door open. A clean exit preserves the referral; nagging destroys it.
4. **No manufactured urgency.** No fake discounts, no "last chance." Vanorika publishes real prices. That's the brand, don't undercut it for one deal.
5. **Make it trivially easy to say no.** People go quiet because saying no is awkward. Remove the awkwardness and you'll often get a yes instead.
6. **No dashes in any of these messages.** `brand-voice.md` rule 11. These go to real people who will spot it.

## Day 3: the second finding

You held findings back in the opener. Spend one now.

> Hi [Name], one more thing came out of that [domain] scan I thought you'd want to know: [SECOND FINDING].
>
> [Business consequence, one line.]
>
> Full breakdown's still yours free whenever you want it, no rush.

---

## Day 10: useful, not salesy

Send something genuinely helpful that happens to be relevant. You have 11 blog posts and ~20 free tools. Use them.

> Hi [Name], wrote this up and thought of you: [link to relevant post].
>
> [One line on why it's relevant *to them specifically*.]
>
> No agenda, just thought it was useful.

Matching guide: WordPress site → "Is Your WordPress Site a Sitting Duck?" · price-shopping → "How Much Does a Penetration Test Cost in Zimbabwe?" · collects customer data → the DPA-2021 compliance checklist.

---

## Day 30: the clean close

> Hi [Name], I'll stop here so I'm not cluttering your phone.
>
> The [domain] report is yours whenever you want it, free, no conditions. If security moves up the list later, you know where I am.
>
> Either way, good luck with [specific thing about their business].
>
> Donovan · +263 77 690 2542

**This message converts.** It removes all pressure, and it's often the one that gets the reply. Mean it. If they say no, actually stop.

## After a consult with no close

Different situation: they've talked to you, so the barrier isn't awareness, it's a specific unresolved objection.

> Hi [Name], thinking about our conversation. I reckon the thing that's actually holding this up is [YOUR HONEST GUESS AT THE REAL OBJECTION].
>
> If that's right, [DIRECT ANSWER TO IT].
>
> If it's something else, tell me straight. I'd rather know than guess.

Naming the objection out loud is disarming, and it beats another round of pleasantries.

## Common objections and honest answers

**"It's too expensive."**
> Fair. What's the number that works? I'd rather scope it down to fit than lose the job. A $150 assessment that finds the serious things beats a $400 test you don't buy.

**"We'll do it later / after [event]."**
> Makes sense. Want me to check back in [month]? The free scan stands whenever.
> *(Then actually diarise it. This is the most common real answer and it's not a brush-off.)*

**"We have someone who handles that."**
> Good, then you don't need me to fix anything. But a second pair of eyes costs you nothing here: I'll send the report, you hand it to them. If it's clean, that's genuinely useful to know.

**"How do I know you're any good?"**
> Reasonable question. Three things you can check yourself: my tools are open source at github.com/panache08/vanorika-security-tools, my own site scores 100% on the same tests I'd run on yours, and there's a redacted sample report on the site so you can see exactly what you'd get. And I'm CompTIA PenTest+ certified.

**"Send me a proposal."** *(often a polite no)*
> Will do. Before I write it, is there anything you already know would be a blocker? Saves us both a round trip.

## The rule

**Log the follow-up date.** Solo founder, no CRM. A follow-up you meant to send is worth nothing, and this is where most of the deals in the current pipeline actually live.

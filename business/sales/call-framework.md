# Sales Call Framework

> The free 20 to 30 minute consult. Step 01 of the delivery process, and where deals are actually won.
> WhatsApp call, Zoom, or in person in Harare. Booking: calendly.com/donovanmudarikwa/30min

---

## The one thing to remember

**You are not there to convince anyone.** You are there to find out whether there's a real problem worth paying to fix, and to be the person who was straight with them.

The scan already did the persuading. The finding is real and they can check it. So the call is diagnosis, not pitching. If there's no real problem, say so and end early. That call costs you 20 minutes and earns you a referral. Talking someone into a $400 test they don't need costs you the business.

---

## Before the call (5 minutes, non-negotiable)

1. **Re-run the scan.** Things change; arriving with a stale finding is embarrassing.
2. **Look at the actual site.** What do they sell? Who buys it? Where's the money?
3. **Pick the one finding that touches revenue.** Not the most severe. The one nearest the cash.
4. **Decide your walk-away.** What's the smallest honest engagement here? Usually a $150 assessment.

---

## Opening (2 min)

Set the frame immediately. It's disarming and it's true:

> Thanks for making time. Quick note on how I run these: I'm not going to pitch you. I want to understand what you've actually got, tell you honestly whether it's a problem, and if it isn't, I'll say so and give you your 20 minutes back. Sound alright?

Then hand over:

> So, tell me about [business]. Where does the business actually come from?

**Then stop talking.** This is the highest-value part of the call and most people fill it with a pitch.

---

## Diagnosis (10 min): most of the call

You're finding out three things: **what they'd lose, who's responsible, and who decides.**

**What's at stake**
- "What would it actually cost you if the site went down for a day?"
- "Do you take payments or hold customer details on there?"
- "Where do enquiries land? The site, WhatsApp, Instagram?"

**Who's responsible**
- "Who built the site? Are you still in touch with them?"
- "When was it last updated?"
- "If something broke tonight, who'd you call?"

**Have they been burned**
- "Has anything gone wrong before? Hacked, down, defaced, dodgy emails?"
- "Anyone ever looked at the security side?"

**Who decides** *(ask it plainly, because it's the most-skipped question and the most expensive to skip)*
- "If this is worth doing, is it your call or does someone else need to sign off?"
- "Roughly what would you expect something like this to cost?"

---

## The finding (3 min)

Now deliver it. **One finding. Plain English. Consequence, then evidence.**

> Here's the thing I'd want you to know about. [FINDING in ordinary words.]
>
> What that means for you is [CONSEQUENCE: in money, customers, or reputation].
>
> You don't have to take my word for it. [How they can check it themselves.]

Then be quiet and let them react. Their reaction tells you whether this is a deal.

**Don't dump the rest.** The other findings are the deliverable. Giving them all away on the call removes the reason to buy, and makes it look like a sales tactic rather than a report.

---

## The ask (5 min)

Only if a real problem surfaced. If it didn't, skip straight to "no problem here" below.

**Present one option, not a menu.** Choice paralyses; a recommendation converts.

> Based on what you've told me, I'd do [SPECIFIC SCOPE]. That's [PRICE], fixed: 50% to start, 50% when you get the report. You'd have it in [5 to 7 business days / 1 to 2 weeks].
>
> You'd get a written report with everything I find, ranked by how much it actually matters to your business. You can have me fix them, or hand it to your developer. Genuinely your choice.
>
> Want me to write that up?

Match the scope honestly: nervous about price → $150 assessment · handles payments/customer data → $400+ pentest · site is genuinely broken → website work first, security after · already fine → the $50/mo retainer, or nothing.

---

## When there's no real problem

**Say it.** This is the highest-return move available to you.

> Honestly? Your setup's in decent shape. There's [minor thing] but it's not urgent and your developer can do it in an afternoon. I wouldn't take your money for this.
>
> If anything changes, or you want a second pair of eyes before a big launch, I'm on WhatsApp.

You just became the security person who told someone *not* to buy. In a referral-driven market, that's worth more than the $150.

---

## Closing every call

> I'll send you the full report either way, free, no conditions. If you want to go ahead, reply and I'll write up the scope.

Then: **send it the same day.** The report is the proof and the follow-up in one.

---

## Never

- **Never invent or inflate a finding to close.** The business is built on findings being real. One inflated report and it's over.
- **Never fear-sell.** No "it's not if but when."
- **Never discount on the call.** Prices are published. Undercutting them says the published price was a lie. Scope down instead. That's honest, and it protects the pricing page.
- **Never quote a number you haven't thought through.** "Let me scope it and send you a fixed price today" is a complete answer.
- **Never promise what one person can't deliver on time.**
- **Never bad-mouth their developer.** They chose them. Attacking the choice attacks them, and half the time the developer is a relative.

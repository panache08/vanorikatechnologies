# DPO Partnership Play

Working document. How Vanorika partners with a licensed Data Protection Officer instead of pretending to be one.

## Why this exists

Phronesis Cybersecurity is selling "DPO as a Service" in Harare from US$200/month, targeting SMEs, NGOs, schools, clinics, hotels, churches, financial services, and corporates. That is a validated market signal: Zimbabwean organisations are now spending money on Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07] compliance.

Vanorika cannot sell the DPO role itself. The role carries registration requirements with POTRAZ, and the work is legal and regulatory rather than technical. What Vanorika can do is the half that compliance firms are worst at: actually building the systems that make the advice real.

That gap is the partnership.

## VERIFY BEFORE PITCHING

Confirm with POTRAZ directly, in writing if possible:

1. Are Data Protection Officers required to be registered or licensed with POTRAZ?
2. What are the current registration requirements and fees?
3. Is there a public register of licensed DPOs? (This is the prospect list.)

Do not send this pitch until question 1 is answered. The entire premise rests on it. If DPOs are not in fact licensed, the partnership still makes sense on expertise grounds, but the pitch changes from "you have something I legally cannot get" to "you have expertise I do not."

## The pitch, in one paragraph

Most DPO engagements end with a policy document and a compliance gap register. The client then has to find someone to build consent capture, fix access control, implement retention, add audit logging, and stand up breach detection. Usually nobody does, so the gap register is still open at the next review. Vanorika is the build arm. You keep the regulatory relationship, the sign-off, and the client. We turn your findings into working software.

## Three commercial structures

Pick based on how much the partner wants to own the client relationship.

**1. Referral, both directions.** Simplest. They refer clients needing technical remediation, we refer clients needing a DPO. 10 to 15 percent referral fee, or straight reciprocity with no money changing hands. Lowest friction, lowest commitment, good first step. Start here.

**2. Subcontract.** They sell a complete compliance package to the client and subcontract the technical build to Vanorika at an agreed rate. They own the client and the margin. We get predictable work with zero sales cost. Good if the partner already has a book of clients.

**3. Joint offer.** A co-branded package with a single price and a split. Something like "Compliance Complete": their DPO retainer plus our readiness assessment and remediation build. Highest revenue per client, requires the most trust, and should only follow one or two successful projects under structure 1 or 2.

Open with 1. Propose 2 if they have clients now. Only raise 3 after delivering something.

## How to find the partner

In rough order of promise:

1. The POTRAZ register of licensed DPOs, if one exists. Direct and unambiguous.
2. Law firms in Harare with a data protection or technology practice. They have the legal side and almost never have the technical side.
3. Accounting and audit firms already selling compliance and risk services to SMEs.
4. Individual consultants advertising DPO services on LinkedIn. Search "Data Protection Officer Zimbabwe".
5. Phronesis themselves. Genuinely worth considering. They are selling the advisory layer at US$200 a month and will hit technical remediation requests they cannot fulfil. A competitor for the retainer is a partner for the build.

## What to say when they ask "why not just get licensed yourself?"

Answer honestly, because it is the truth and it builds the trust the partnership needs: it is on the table. If the market proves out, registration is a 6 to 12 month path. But it takes longer to become a credible DPO than it takes to help their clients this quarter, and a partner delivering now is worth more to both of us than a competitor arriving next year.
